# Equality of Matrices
==2024-12-03

---
## Theory

Two matrices A = $[a_{ij}]_{m_{a}\times n_{a}}$, B = $[b_{ij}]_{m_{b}\times n_{b}}$ are said to be equal in following condition:
$$A=B\iff(m_{a}=m_{b} \text{ } \Lambda \text{ } n_{a}=n_{b})\text{ }\Lambda\text{ }(a_{ij}=b_{ij})$$
---
## Examples

Given,
$$
A=\begin{bmatrix}
1&2&3 \\
2&3&4 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$
$$
B=\begin{bmatrix}
1&2&3 \\
2&3&4 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$
Then, $A=B$.

---
Given,
$$
A=\begin{bmatrix}
9&7&5 \\
1&9&8 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$
$$
B=\begin{bmatrix}
1&2&3 \\
2&3&4 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$
Then $A\neq B$ because corresponding elements aren't equal.

---
Given,
$$
A=\begin{bmatrix}
9&7&5 \\
1&9&8 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$
$$
B=\begin{bmatrix}
1&2&3 \\
\end{bmatrix}
$$
Then $A\neq B$ because order of the matrices aren't equal.
